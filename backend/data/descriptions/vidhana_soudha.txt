Vidhana Soudha is the seat of Karnataka's state legislature, located on Ambedkar Veedhi in the heart of Bengaluru. Constructed between 1952 and 1956 under the leadership of Chief Minister Kengal Hanumanthaiah, it stands as one of the most impressive examples of Neo-Dravidian architecture in modern India. The building was designed to symbolize the power of democracy and the sovereignty of the people of Karnataka.

Built entirely from Bengaluru granite, Vidhana Soudha is a four-storey structure featuring massive Dravidian pillars, ornamental domes, and a grand central stairway. The imposing facade stretches over 700 feet and incorporates traditional South Indian temple motifs into a modern legislative building. The inscription "Government's Work is God's Work" adorns the entrance in Kannada.

The building sits adjacent to the Attara Kacheri (High Court) and is a short walk from Cubbon Park and the State Central Library. Vidhana Soudha is beautifully illuminated on Sundays and public holidays, making it a landmark visible across the city skyline. It remains one of Bengaluru's most photographed monuments and a symbol of Karnataka's post-independence identity.
