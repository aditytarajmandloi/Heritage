The State Central Library, also known as Seshadri Iyer Memorial Hall, is a striking Neo-Classical building situated within the lush greenery of Cubbon Park in central Bengaluru. Established in 1915 and named after Sir K. Seshadri Iyer, the Diwan of Mysore, it is one of India's oldest and largest public libraries. The building was constructed under the direction of the British colonial administration as a public reading room and repository of knowledge.

The library's architecture features a distinctive red-painted facade with Corinthian columns, symmetrical arched windows, and a grand pediment entrance that exemplifies the Neo-Classical style popular in British India. The building's formal proportions and classical ornamentation reflect the European design sensibilities imposed on Indian civic architecture during the colonial period.

Located at the heart of Cubbon Park, the library is surrounded by botanical gardens, walking paths, and heritage trees that are over a century old. It sits in close proximity to other significant landmarks including the Vidhana Soudha, the Attara Kacheri (High Court), and the Government Museum. The library holds over 2.5 lakh books in multiple languages including Kannada, English, Hindi, and Sanskrit. It remains a beloved public institution and an architectural gem of Bengaluru's civic heritage.
