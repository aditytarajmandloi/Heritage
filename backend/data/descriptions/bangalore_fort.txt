Bangalore Fort is a historic fortification located near KR Market in Old Bengaluru. Originally built as a mud fort by Kempe Gowda I, the founder of Bengaluru, in 1537 during the Vijayanagara Empire period, it marks the very birthplace of the city. The fort was later strengthened and rebuilt in stone by Hyder Ali in 1761, and further expanded by his son Tipu Sultan, who added bastions, a moat, and additional defensive structures.

The fort originally had thick stone walls and multiple gates, but much of the structure was demolished by the British after their victory at the Siege of Seringapatam in 1799. Today, only the Delhi Gate (named because it faced the direction of Delhi) and a small section of the fort walls survive as protected monuments. The remaining gateway features Indo-Islamic arched doorways, bastions with loopholes for muskets, and inscriptions from both the Hyder Ali and British periods.

The fort is located in close proximity to Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace, which was built within the fort grounds. Together, they form the most significant pre-colonial heritage cluster in Bengaluru, telling the story of the city's transition from a Vijayanagara market town to a Mysorean stronghold to a British cantonment. The fort is maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India as a monument of national importance.
