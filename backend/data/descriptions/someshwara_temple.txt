The Someshwara Temple in Halasuru (Ulsoor) is widely regarded as the oldest surviving temple in Bengaluru, with origins dating back to the Chola dynasty around 1085 CE. Dedicated to Lord Shiva (as Someshwara, the Lord of the Moon), this temple is a fine example of Chola-Dravidian architecture and has been continuously renovated and expanded over nearly a millennium by successive dynasties including the Hoysalas, Vijayanagara rulers, and the Wodeyars of Mysore.

The temple complex features a towering gopuram (gateway tower) richly adorned with stucco figures of deities, a spacious pillared mandapam (hall) with intricately carved columns, and a central sanctum housing the Shiva Linga. The pillars display a mix of Chola-period simplicity and later Vijayanagara-era elaboration, reflecting the temple's layered history. Stone inscriptions found in the complex date to various periods and provide valuable historical records of Bengaluru's early settlement.

Located in Halasuru, one of Bengaluru's oldest neighborhoods, the Someshwara Temple remains an active place of worship and a cultural anchor for the local community. It hosts vibrant festivals including Maha Shivaratri and Karthikai Deepam. The temple's survival through nearly a thousand years of dynastic changes makes it the most historically significant religious structure in the city.
