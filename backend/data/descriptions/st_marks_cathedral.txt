St. Mark's Cathedral is one of the oldest Anglican churches in Asia, located on MG Road in central Bengaluru. The cathedral was consecrated in 1808 during the early years of British rule in Bengaluru, making it over two centuries old. It was built under the supervision of Colonel J. J. Guthrie to serve the British military and civilian community stationed at the Bengaluru Cantonment.

The cathedral is built in the Gothic Revival architectural style, featuring a tall steeple, pointed arched windows, buttressed walls, and elegant stained glass panels depicting biblical scenes. The interior has a classic nave-and-aisle layout with wooden pews, a raised altar, and memorial plaques commemorating British soldiers and administrators who served in Bengaluru. The building's stonework and steeple create a striking silhouette against the modern skyline of MG Road.

The cathedral grounds include a peaceful cemetery with gravestones dating back to the early 19th century, offering a quiet respite from the bustle of one of Bengaluru's busiest commercial streets. St. Mark's Cathedral remains an active place of worship and hosts regular services, choral performances, and heritage walks. It is located near Mayo Hall and forms part of the MG Road heritage cluster alongside the Government Museum, Cubbon Park, and the Attara Kacheri.
