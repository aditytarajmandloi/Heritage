Mayo Hall is a prominent Neo-Classical heritage building located on MG Road in central Bengaluru. Built in 1883, it was named in memory of Lord Mayo (Richard Southwell Bourke), the 4th Viceroy of India, who was assassinated in 1872 during a visit to the Andaman Islands. The hall was constructed during the height of the British Raj and served as a venue for public meetings, social gatherings, and administrative functions.

The building showcases typical Neo-Classical architectural features including a symmetrical facade, tall Corinthian columns, arched windows, and an elevated entrance with a grand portico. The structure is built from locally quarried stone and features a central hall with high ceilings. Its dignified yet restrained colonial aesthetic makes it a fine example of institutional architecture from the British period in Bengaluru.

Today, Mayo Hall continues to serve as a public building under the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP), the city's municipal corporation. It is located near St. Mark's Cathedral and the Government Museum, forming part of the MG Road heritage cluster in central Bengaluru. The building stands as a reminder of the city's colonial history alongside the more contemporary developments along the bustling MG Road corridor.
