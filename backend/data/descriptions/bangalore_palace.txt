Bangalore Palace is a stunning Tudor Revival-style palace located in Vasanth Nagar, in the heart of Bengaluru. Built in 1878 by King Chamaraja Wadiyar of the Mysore royal family, the palace was inspired by England's Windsor Castle after the young prince visited the English countryside. The architect John Cameron designed it with fortified towers, battlements, and Gothic-style windows that evoke medieval English grandeur.

The palace grounds span 454 acres and feature lush gardens, ornamental woodwork, and elegant interiors decorated with floral motifs, Victorian-era furnishings, and carved wooden ceilings. The Tudor-style half-timbered facade, combined with granite turrets and crenellated parapets, creates a striking silhouette that feels transported from the English countryside to tropical South India.

Inside, visitors can explore royal ballrooms, hunting trophies, and rare oil paintings from the Wadiyar dynasty. The grounds frequently host large-scale music concerts and cultural events. The palace stands as one of the few Tudor Revival structures in India and serves as a fascinating contrast to Bengaluru's predominantly Dravidian and colonial architecture. It remains privately owned by the Mysore royal family descendants but is open to public tours.
