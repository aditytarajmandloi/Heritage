The Government Museum of Bengaluru, also known as the Venkatappa Art Gallery and Museum complex, is located on Kasturba Road in central Bengaluru. Established in 1886, it is one of the oldest museums in India and the second oldest in South India after the Chennai Government Museum. The museum is housed in a grand Neo-Classical building featuring symmetrical facades, Corinthian columns, and a dignified entrance that reflects the British colonial architectural traditions of the late 19th century.

The museum's collection spans several departments: geology, art, numismatics, sculpture, and natural history. Notable exhibits include 12th-century Hoysala and Vijayanagara stone sculptures, rare coins from the Gangas, Chalukyas, and Kadambas, and archaeological artifacts dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization, including Mohenjo-daro relics. The Venkatappa Art Gallery wing showcases the works of K. Venkatappa, a celebrated court painter of the Mysore Kingdom.

Situated inside Cubbon Park and adjacent to the State Central Library and the Attara Kacheri (High Court), the Government Museum is a cornerstone of Bengaluru's cultural precinct. The museum offers a compact yet rich journey through Karnataka's history, from prehistoric times through medieval dynasties to the colonial era.
